Workflow

Capture. Analyze. Correct. Track.

The full SmartSwing loop is designed to shorten the time between a technical problem and the practice behavior that fixes it. Less friction. Less ambiguity. More useful reps.

Outcome

Speed to correction is the product.

Every surface should help the player understand what happened, why it matters, and what to do next.

01

Record the session

The player selects the shot, mode, and goal first. That context shapes the scoring logic, benchmarks, and the kind of report language they will see afterward.

Pre-capture guidance improves input quality before the upload starts.
Shot and session intent define the benchmark and feedback frame.
Profile details keep the scoring fair by level and player context.
02

Run the AI analyzer

The analyzer tracks body landmarks, computes the most relevant stroke metrics, and converts those signals into a readable report that does not drown the player in raw mechanics jargon.

Biomechanics, movement, positioning, timing, and contact behavior are assessed together.
The report emphasizes top priorities instead of overwhelming low-value detail.
Expandable cards explain what a metric means and how to interpret it.
03

Get a focused plan

The report narrows the next step to the most meaningful drill plans and tactical adjustments. That keeps improvement tangible and makes the score feel connected to real practice.

Top 3 drill recommendations tied directly to the main weaknesses.
Top 3 tactical recommendations based on the player's current profile.
Coach-ready summary keeps the teaching conversation fast and useful.
04

Track, message, schedule

Once the report exists, the work moves into dashboards. That is where assignments, progress, scheduling, coach communication, and access guardrails keep the loop alive.

Player and coach views are separated into clean tabs by task.
Plan limits, trials, and privacy rules are enforced before save, export, and messaging actions.
Players and coaches stay in their own scoped views so communication and next actions remain easy to follow.
